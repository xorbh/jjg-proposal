We're excited about the opportunity to partner with JJG Manufacturing on integrating AI across your operations. Based on our initial discussion, we've outlined two engagement models below. Each reflects a different balance of flexibility and structure — we'd love your feedback on which feels like the right fit, or whether a hybrid makes sense.

## Option A: Integrated Monthly Retainer (Advisory + Development)

This model bundles strategic advisory and a dedicated pool of development hours into a single monthly retainer. It's designed for speed — when we spot an opportunity, we can move straight into building without the overhead of scoping a separate project each time.

### How It Works

**Month 1 — Discovery & Orientation.** The first month is focused entirely on understanding your operations, data landscape, and AI readiness across departments. We conduct stakeholder interviews, observe shop floor processes, review existing systems, and identify the highest-impact opportunities. This month produces an initial AI opportunity map and sets priorities for the months ahead.

**Month 2 Onward — Advisory + Development.** From the second month, the retainer expands to include a fixed pool of development hours alongside the ongoing advisory. Each month, a portion of the retainer covers strategic guidance — regular check-ins with your teams, use case identification, vendor evaluation, and change management support. The remaining portion covers hands-on development work: building prototypes, running pilots, integrating AI models, and iterating on solutions. The advisory and development work run in parallel — as we identify opportunities through our ongoing engagement, we can begin implementing immediately using the included development hours.

**Scaling Up.** If a particular initiative requires more development capacity than the base retainer provides, we can bring in additional resources on a time-and-materials basis. This keeps your baseline cost predictable while giving you the flexibility to accelerate when needed.

### Why This Model

Our experience with manufacturing clients is that AI opportunities surface gradually — a department may not know what's possible until they see what worked next door. The traditional approach of scoping and pricing each initiative separately creates friction and delays. By bundling development into the retainer, we can move from "we should try this" to "here's a working prototype" within the same month. The discovery-first month ensures we're not building blindly — we invest the first few weeks understanding your operations so the development hours that follow are well-targeted.

### What You Get

A predictable, even monthly cost that covers both the thinking and the doing. You get a consistent AI partner embedded in your operations — not just advising, but actively building. As we learn your business more deeply each month, the development work becomes increasingly well-aimed. And because the monthly cost is fixed, budgeting and internal approvals are straightforward.

### Rate Structure

The retainer is billed as a single fixed monthly fee. Month 1 (discovery) is billed at a reduced rate reflecting the advisory-only scope. From Month 2 onward, the fee includes both advisory time and a defined number of development hours per month. Any development work exceeding the included hours would be billed on a time-and-materials basis at pre-agreed rates. Travel and third-party tooling or infrastructure costs would be billed separately. The retainer can be scaled up (more development hours) or down by mutual agreement, typically with one month's notice.

## Option B: Phased Engagement with Formal Deliveries

This model follows a structured sequence of phases, each with defined deliverables and decision points. It works well when you prefer clear milestones, formal sign-offs, and a roadmap you can socialize internally.

### How It Works

**Phase 1 — Discovery & Assessment (4–6 weeks)**
We conduct a thorough assessment across your departments to understand operations, data landscape, pain points, and AI readiness. This involves stakeholder interviews, shop floor observation, data infrastructure review, and benchmarking against industry practices. The phase concludes with a formal deliverable: an AI Opportunity Roadmap that prioritizes use cases by impact, feasibility, and data readiness.

**Phase 2 — Pilot Design & Proof of Concept (8–12 weeks)**
Based on the roadmap, we select 1–2 high-priority use cases and build working prototypes. This phase includes detailed solution design documents, data pipeline setup, model development, and validation against your operational metrics. Each pilot concludes with a documented evaluation — what worked, what didn't, and a go/no-go recommendation for production deployment.

**Phase 3 — Production Deployment & Integration (timeline varies)**
Approved pilots move into production. This phase covers system integration, operator training, monitoring setup, and a defined hypercare period. Each deployment has its own project plan with milestones and acceptance criteria.

**Phase 4 — Expansion & Optimization (ongoing)**
With initial deployments stable, we return to the roadmap and scope the next wave of use cases. This phase can repeat as many times as needed, drawing on lessons learned from earlier phases.

### Why This Model

Manufacturing environments — especially in automotive — often require formal documentation, internal approvals, and clear accountability at each stage. This model gives your leadership and department heads visibility into what's happening, what it costs, and what comes next. It also creates natural pause points where you can decide whether to continue, pivot, or pause.

### What You Get

Each phase produces tangible deliverables you can review, share internally, and use to build organizational buy-in. The phased structure also means you're never more than one phase's commitment away from a decision point.

### Rate Structure

Discovery and assessment would typically be scoped as a fixed-fee engagement. Subsequent phases (pilot, deployment, expansion) would be scoped individually — either as fixed-fee or time-and-materials depending on the complexity and certainty of requirements. Each phase would be quoted and approved before work begins. Travel and third-party costs would be billed separately.

## Comparing the Two Models

| Dimension | Option A: Integrated Monthly Retainer | Option B: Phased Delivery |
|-----------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Flexibility** | High — scope evolves organically each month | Moderate — structured but with decision gates |
| **Predictability** | Even monthly cost; overflow billed at agreed rates | Each phase is quoted upfront |
| **Speed to first value** | Fast — development starts from Month 2 | Moderate — discovery phase comes first |
| **Internal visibility** | Lighter documentation, monthly progress updates | Formal deliverables at each gate |
| **Commitment model** | Month-to-month with scaling flexibility | Phase-by-phase |
| **Best suited for** | Organizations that want continuous discovery and delivery | Organizations that need structured milestones and approvals |

---

## Our Recommendation

Both models are proven approaches and we're comfortable executing either. That said, we find that the best choice often depends on how your internal decision-making works. If your teams have the autonomy to move quickly and you want AI embedded as an ongoing capability with development happening continuously, Option A tends to work well. If you need formal business cases and stage-gate approvals — which is common and entirely reasonable in automotive manufacturing — Option B may be a better cultural fit.

We're also open to a hybrid: for example, starting with a structured discovery phase (from Option B) and then transitioning to a retainer model (from Option A) once priorities are clear.
