01
Approach

A Two-Phase
Engagement

  • Month 1 — Discovery only. We spend the first month understanding your operations, systems, and data. No building yet. This produces a prioritised roadmap.
  • Months 2–6 — Consulting + Delivery. Based on what we find, we move into building. Each month: a small consulting component to guide priorities, and hands-on delivery of 1–2 projects.
  • All work billed on a time-and-materials basis
02
Month 1 — Discovery Only

Discovery &
Opportunity Mapping

  • Walk the floor — understand how things actually work across production, quality, maintenance, and planning
  • Review existing systems, data sources, and manual processes
  • Assess data readiness — what's available, what's reliable, where the gaps are
  • Deliver a prioritised opportunity map that guides everything that follows

This month is consulting only — 20 hours. No development. The output is the roadmap that shapes all subsequent work.

03
Months 2–6 — Consulting + Delivery

1–2 Projects
Per Month

Each month includes 5 hours of consulting to refine priorities, plus 30 hours of hands-on delivery. Examples of the kind of things we'd build:

  • CEO / MD dashboard — natural language querying of business data ("What drove scrap last month?")
  • Invoice processing — extract and classify data from invoices automatically
  • Demand forecasting — predict inventory needs to reduce over/understocking
  • Vendor management — summarise contracts, flag renewal dates, compare bids
  • Meeting summarisation — auto-generate action items from calls
  • Process documentation — auto-generate SOPs from recorded workflows
  • Anomaly detection — flag unusual transactions, production deviations, or maintenance patterns
  • CRM auto-fill — automatically log call notes, emails, and next steps

These are illustrative. Actual projects are determined by the Month 1 discovery.

05
Why This Model

The Real
Bottleneck

  • In the AI era, development speed is rarely the constraint
  • The bottleneck is usually clarifications — understanding requirements, getting answers, resolving ambiguity
  • And adoption — availability of your teams to review, test, and start using what's built
  • That's why we've proposed a medium-sized model for Months 2–6: enough delivery capacity to keep moving, without outrunning your team's ability to absorb

We can scale up. If adoption is fast and your teams have bandwidth, we offer more involved models with higher consulting and delivery hours.

Built by Kwartz

Our
Tools

  • Gatekeeper — centralized access control for AI-connected data. Decides who can query what, with zero friction for end users. One dashboard to manage privileges across all data sources — no one sees more than they should.
  • Cadence — automate and run business processes with built-in approvals and access management. Designed for organisations that don't have large IT teams — workflows, escalations, and audit trails out of the box.
